# Stage 6: Breaking the "Coward Policy" - Asymmetric Risk-Reward Tuning

In the Stage 5 (v3) experiments, the evaluation protocol correctly decoupled the tactical posture from the intent conditioning. However, the results revealed an unexpected issue: MAPPO achieved better collision rates (e.g., 0.21 vs I-MAPPO's 0.33 in standard eval) by adopting an "Ultimate Coward" policy. Because MAPPO cannot understand the tactical posture, it avoids the radar zones entirely and stays on the safe periphery, sacrificing aggressive task execution for a trivially low collision rate. Meanwhile, I-MAPPO aggressively pursues targets during "Attack" phases, which naturally increases its local collision probability.

To prove that I-MAPPO's intent-driven policy is superior in overall tactical effectiveness, we must fundamentally reshape the risk-reward imbalance in the environment. We need to force agents to take risks to achieve high returns, heavily penalize lingering, and allow high-value rewards to bypass the strict clipping.

## Task 1: High-Value Targets inside Radar Zones
We must strongly incentivize UAVs to enter the radar zones during the `Attack` posture.
Please modify the `reward()` function in `uav_scheduling_env.py`:
1. **Aggressive Bonus:** If a UAV enters a Radar Threat Zone (distance to a target < `R_threat`) while the environment's `current_tactical_posture == 'attack'`, grant a massive positive reward (e.g., `reward_task += 5.0`).
2. **Stealth Penalty:** If a UAV enters the Radar Threat Zone while the posture is `'stealth'`, keep the existing massive lethal penalty (e.g., `reward -= 5.0`).
*Logic:* I-MAPPO will learn to enter the zone ONLY when ordered, reaping massive rewards. MAPPO will either stay out (getting low task rewards) or guess blindly (getting destroyed by the lethal penalty).
